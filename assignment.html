Algorithms

An algorithm is a set of instructions or sequence of steps followed to accomplish a desired outcome. Although these calculations are normally performed by a computer, a programmer is the hero who creates these instructions.

By 2025, 80 percent of the functions doctors do will be done much better and much more cheaply by machines and machine learned algorithms. -Vinod Khosla

Algorithm Categories

While there is a myriad of algorithms to peruse, here are some prominent types of algorithms very beneficial to understand the concept of: recursive, divide and conquer, dynamic programming, greedy, brute force, and backtracking.

An algorithm can be represented using a flowchart to illustrate the individual steps.

Analysis of Feedback Control Algorithm

The AIMD, Additive Increase-Multiplicative Decrease, algorithm is very useful when congestion of data hampers TCP (transmission control protocol). It works by increasing the flow of data/packets exponentially until an acknowledgement of a packet goes undetected. Once confirmed, not only does it half the flow of data/packets but also alters the increase in data/packets from 'exponentially' to 'plus 1.'
